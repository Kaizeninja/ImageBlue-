    Aside from the addition of the FedEx logo to the jersey, the unveiling of new uniforms, logos and color schemes did not reveal any dramatic overhaul of the existing aesthetic for the Memphis Grizzlies. 

    The colors and overall look of the team's uniforms are largely similar to the previous scheme. Although, there is at least one change to the branding that might go unnoticed to some. 

    Buy Photo

    Memphis Grizzlies guard Mike Conley speaks to reporters Thursday after the team unveiled a refreshed logo and color scheme at FedExForum. (Photo: Mark Weber/The Commercial Appeal)

    But not us. 

    From 2004-18, the Grizzlies' "clawball" logo featured a right bear claw wrapped around a basketball. The new version has flipped the script. Now, a left bear claw has a firm grasp of a yellow basketball. 

    After struggling with a cutter for 14 years, the Memphis Grizzlies have scrapped the pitch in favor of a traditional four-seam fastball. They now also throw with the other hand, apparently... (@sportslogosnet) pic.twitter.com/9FIhlQCWkG

    — Ben Harris (@byBenHarris) August 2, 2018

    And, wouldn't you know, the alteration comes just in time for International Left-handers Day, which is set for Aug. 13. 

    In honor of the subtle difference and the upcoming observance of left-handers, here are a few facts about southpaws you may not have known: 

    — Left-handers make up roughly 10 percent of the world's population. 
    — Left-handedness is about twice as common in twins than in the general population
    — At least four of the last seven U.S. presidents have been left-handed
